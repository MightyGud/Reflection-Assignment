Design ideas

My ideas for the design itself are guided by 3 main values: immersive, playful, and curiosity. I wanted users to be drawn into this immersive feeling of a landscape where they can explore and experience the UI without getting the feeling of boredom, and be more persuaded. That's how I came up with a cosmic landscape where you can explore and interact with the planets. Each planet represents a note and plays when the users click it. And the playfulness will be generated by dragging the planets by using Math.random(). When you drag one planet, it generates a random note. The curiosity was encouraged by hiding the interactive elements within the visual space. And it can be done by moving or dragging.

Feedback, Information, and Learnability.

For the feedback is when a user drags or clicks on one of the chosen planets, when clicked, a tone will play from the planet, and when held, the earth stops and follows the cursor hand, while moving it will generate random notes and only stop when released. One of the feedback that I want is that when you hover around the planet, the planet will zoom out by itself, which also shows that you can interact with the planet and explore what each planet has.

The Information of the design is to avoid as much text as possible, and more interaction and exploration. The system itself is like a map that is meant to direct the users to follow the flow of the design. This concept of mapping styles helps users feel like they are playing instead of just looking around with nothing else to do. And by interaction with the planet, this allows the design to be more interesting to the users, so that they can use these planets to generate notes.

Learnability will come naturally through play. I want to aim for no instructions. Instead of the instructions, this will be more of a user's experiment, by receiving instant feedback, and understanding how the movement relates to the audio. Each action builds a familiar vibe and rewarding rather than instruction.
